### About this event

In 2011, researchers interested in complexity and networks formed a coalition at the University of Cambridge. Scientists from across departments organized a variety of events and talks to exchange ideas and foster collaborations around these topics. This successful initiative continued for several years, but became difficult to maintain during the pandemic.

This event marks a relaunch of the group, with the aim of reconnecting previous participants and welcoming new ones interested in complex networks. The event is open to everyone, whether it relates directly to your research or is simply an area of curiosity.

##### Programme
- `14:00 – 14:30:`  Reception and arrival
- `14:30 – 15:00:`  Welcome talk and introduction
- `15:00 – 16:00:`  Short talks from the organizing committee
- - Dr. George Cantwell: Random Processes on Complex Networks
- - Prof. Sebastian Ahnert: Tudor Networks of Power
- - Prof. Petra Vértes: Connecting Structure and Function in Brain Networks
- `16:00 – 17:00:`  Tea and informal discussion

_The event is free to attend, but registration is required. Capacity is limited and, if oversubscribed, places will be allocated on a first-come, first-served basis. We will nevertheless do our best to accommodate everyone._
