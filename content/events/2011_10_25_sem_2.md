title: "Knotty-Centredness: Finding the Connective Core of a Complex Network"
date: 2011-10-25
time: ["15:00", "15:30"]
location: ["King's College Cambridge","Keynes Hall"]
speaker: "Prof. Murray Shanahan"
affiliation: ["Imperial College London","Department of Computing"]
---

Professor Shanahan presents knotty-centredness, a measure capturing the extent to which a network contains a densely interconnected and topologically central core. Applications to brain networks are highlighted, including analysis of a newly constructed connectome of the pigeon forebrain.
