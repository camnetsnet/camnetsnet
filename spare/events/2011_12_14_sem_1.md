Recent studies suggest that every biological state can be characterized by a gene expression signatureâ€”a defined set of genes and their specific pattern of expression. This principle applies to disease phenotypes, pathway activity, or cellular responses to drugs. While individual genes may poorly capture a state, sets of genes considered together can robustly describe it.

Dr Iorio presents MANTRA (Mode of Action by NeTwoRk Analysis), a computational tool for analyzing the mode of action (MoA) of novel drugs and identifying candidates for drug repurposing. MANTRA integrates network-theory concepts and non-parametric statistics to leverage gene expression patterns for both discovery and repositioning of therapeutics.
